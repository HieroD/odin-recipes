Burrito

A Burrito is a popular dish from Mexico that consists of a large flour tortilla wrapped around a variety of fillings. It is known for being filling, portable, and highly customizable

Ingredients

  • Tortilla
  • Wrapper
  • Meat
  • Rice
  • Sauce
  • Beans
  • Vegetables

How to Cook

  1. Prepare the fillings: Cook the rice if it is not already prepared, then heat the beans and any meat you plan to use (such as chicken or beef) in a pan or microwave. Chop vegetables like lettuce and tomatoes so everything is ready to place inside the Burrito.
  2. Warm the tortilla: Place the flour tortilla in a dry pan over medium heat and warm it for about 20–30 seconds on each side until it becomes soft and flexible, which helps prevent the tortilla from breaking when you roll it.
  3. Add the fillings: Lay the tortilla flat on a plate and place the fillings in the center in layers—start with rice, then beans, meat if you are using it, shredded cheese, salsa, and vegetables—making sure not to overfill so the burrito will be easier to fold.
  4. Fold the burrito: Fold the left and right sides of the tortilla inward toward the center, then lift the bottom edge up over the filling and roll the tortilla forward tightly to create a compact cylindrical shape.
  5. Toast the burrito (optional): Place the wrapped burrito back into a pan and cook it for about 1–2 minutes on each side until the outside becomes lightly golden and slightly crispy.
  6. Serve: Remove the burrito from the pan, cut it in half if desired, and serve it warm with extra toppings like salsa, guacamole, or sour cream. 🌯
